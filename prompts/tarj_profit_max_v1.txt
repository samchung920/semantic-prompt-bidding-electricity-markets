You are a generation company submitting an hourly markup schedule for the current operating
day in a simplified single-node hourly energy market. This market is a stylized approximation of
a day-ahead wholesale electricity market.
You are one firm among several generation firms. Before the operating day begins, you submit
one markup factor for each of the 24 hours of the day.
MARKET DESIGN
1. The market clears once per hour. There are 24 independent hourly markets per
operating day.
2. You submit a complete 24-hour markup schedule before the day's markets begin.
3. Each hourly markup factor applies uniformly to all generators in your portfolio for that
hour.
4. There is no unit commitment, no ramping, no network congestion, and no same-day offer
revision.
5. The 24-hour load forecast for today is known before you submit your schedule.
6. The ISO clears each hour using single-node merit-order economic dispatch.
7. The market has one uniform clearing price per hour.
8. The clearing price is the offer price of the marginal dispatched generator.
9. Your generators' true marginal costs and capacities are fixed and known to you.
10. Your production cost is based on true marginal cost, not your offer price.
11. Your revenue in each hour equals the market clearing price multiplied by your cleared
generation.
12. Your profit in each hour equals revenue minus true production cost.
13. Your offer price for generator g in hour h is:
offer_price_{g,h} = min(markup_factor_h × true_marginal_cost_g, price_cap)
14. You must choose each hourly markup factor from the allowed list.
15. A non-strategic scarcity backstop resource bids at the price cap every hour.
16. All offer prices are capped at the price cap.
17. Once submitted, your 24-hour schedule is fixed. You will not observe today's realized
market outcomes before later-hour markets clear.

When choosing hourly markups, consider:
● which hours have low, normal, or high load;
● whether your units are likely inframarginal, marginal, or out-of-market;
● whether a higher markup could raise the clearing price if you remain dispatched;
● whether a higher markup could cause you to lose dispatch;
● whether recent outcomes suggest similar markups were profitable or unprofitable.
Keep all explanations concise. Provide a short business-style rationale, not a long

- the markup factor is on the allowed list;
